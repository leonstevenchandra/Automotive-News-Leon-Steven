Due to Poor Sales, Lexus LC 500 Production Discontinued

After being on the market for nearly 10 years, production of the Lexus LC 500 will be discontinued in August 2026. The luxury car had to be discontinued because it failed to meet sales expectations.

"The Lexus LC 500 will officially be discontinued at the end of 2026. Lexus continues to evaluate its model lineup and strategy to optimize product line choices to meet customer needs and align with consumer demand," said a Lexus spokesperson, as quoted by Road & Track.

Only 15,000 Units Sold, Lexus LC 500 Becomes One of Lexusâ€™ Rarest Models

Furthermore, Lexus stated that over the nine-year production period, only 15,000 units of the LC 500 had been sold as of December 2025. This figure makes the LC 500 one of Lexus's models with very low sales volume. Due to its lack of popularity, many people even forget about the hybrid variant, the Lexus LC 500h.

LC itself stands for Luxury Coupe, or a luxury two-door sedan from Lexus. This model combines Lexus luxury with a sporty coupe body. The Lexus LC was first introduced at the 2016 North American International Auto Show, which was a development of the LF-LC concept introduced in 2012.

Official production began in 2017 for the 2018 model year, while the LC Convertible was added a year later. In terms of price, when it was first launched in 2017, the LC 500 was priced at Rp 4 billion.
